When I started this website, this blog, it was more or a less a sign to myself and the world that I was taking my creative life 'more seriously'. Not that I intended to take the fun, the creativity, out of anything. Nor was I wanting to make this any significant sort of income, but in the sense that I was starting to work on projects that I could really see putting in front of an audience.

Those of you who know me personally are probably thinking of my art right now- and that's fair. A fair amount of that has been in around in one form or another, including a Print-on-Demand website, but never my writing. That fully journey is for another post, but the long and short of it is that I was starting to see a world where I was a published author! I still do see it, this is by no means a sign that I've given up on that. Its all going better than I expected as I learn how to edit a novel the hard way!

But I digress- what is that picture above? Well, its my first published work!

And its not a book.

I know, it surprised me too.

Okay, it might be more surprising to me than some of you, but as much as I have enjoyed exploring and \*playing \*in the space of solo-play table top games, I had quickly dismissed it as a format I wanted to explore creating in. I just wasn't drawn to the type of research I felt it would need to do anything justice. And I didn't really have any ideas.

No ideas until I literally had one come to me in a dream. The title at least.

"*So You Inherited An Evil Science Lair*" was a phrase that came to me in the world between sleep and wakefulness and refused to leave me alone. As happens with my projects I knew a bit of what this was, and what it wasn't. This was clearly not meant to be the title of a story, or something I was called to make an art piece about. No, this was very clearly a title of a game, one in the style of many that have popped up on the web fairly recently (most commonly on the indie site [itch.io](http://itch.io/) which is also what I would utilise later for my own game). That is, a journaling-adjacent game meant for a person to play on their own, often with playing cards, or dice, or as in my case, the tarot, to add some random elements to the game.

From the moment I put together the tarot (which is another passion of mine so that was no hardship) and the title, it all came together fairly quickly. Like, within a week or two, quickly, which is always a heady feeling.

A bit of playtesting later and I found myself making my first ever sale page. You can see it for yourself by clicking that cover page up there. Its not really anything special, not compared to some of the games with knock-out custom pages, but momentous none the less.

My first publication.

What a thing to say, really, leaves me at a loss for words when I think about it.

So I guess, could you go check it out? Even if it isn't you thing (and I get that, its a niche within a niche), but I'm pretty heckin proud of it, and in the world of algorithm internet, just looking at something can mean a lot.

Okay and I want people to see the things I make, lets be honest that's that whole point of this site, right?
